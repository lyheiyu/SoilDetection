WP3 Baseline Questionnaire (for farmer interviews & yield monitoring)

Purpose: socio-economic impact assessment through mixed methods; align with LL support and ethics/GDPR. （目的：社会经济影响评估；与 LL 支持和伦理/GDPR 对齐）

Introduction

  • Explain the purpose and confirm voluntary participation; clarify confidentiality and access to aggregated results.
  • State linkage to SOILCRATES WP3; clarify no effect on FSTP support; follow project ethics & data protection.

Farming Practices

General crop information

  • Main crops grown; area per crop (ha).
  • Annual crop calendar (planting, maintenance, harvest months).
  • Row spacing / planting density.

Livestock information

  • Animal types; number of animals per ha.
  • Dairy: liters/cow; Meat: weight at slaughter.

Crop & variety selection

  • Criteria & key factors; satisfaction with last year’s choices and reasons.

Agroecological practices

  • Which practices (cover crops, intercropping, reduced tillage, composting, etc.) and why.
  • Since when; details on intercropping patterns; use of cover crops and integration.

Soil inputs

  • Amendments used (compost, manure, synthetic fertilizer, mulch, other).
  • Timing, current/planned season usage, estimated costs, rationale.

Irrigation

  • Use & system type (drip, manual, other); timing (months); annual cost; rationale.

Pest & disease management

  • Chemical or natural; types; timing; annual cost; rationale.

Land preparation & machinery

  • Preparation technique; tillage; machinery; rotations; prep time; costs/resources; rationale.
  • Satisfaction reflection: what worked, what didn’t.

Experience of Decisions During the Season

Climate & agronomic challenges

  • Drought, waterlogging/flooding, high temperatures, other weather challenges.
  • Greatest challenge this season.

Pest challenges

  • Pests faced; most difficult; alternative preparation.

Perceptions of Agroecology

  • Definition (include SOILCRATES definition).
  • Profitability impacts; ambitions for adopting new practices.

Business Model of the Farm

Crop sales & income

  • Crops sold; most profitable; sales channels.
  • Average sale prices; total farm income; price trends vs. 2024.

Agricultural productivity

  • Average yields per crop; potential yields; yield gap; storage loss %.

Farm structure & business model

  • Structure (co-op, independent); main income; labels (organic/other); processing.
  • CAPEX (machinery, irrigation); OPEX (seeds, fuel, labour, amendments).

Profitability & policy

  • Profitability of soil health practices; contribution to resilience & yields.
  • Agroecology as contributor; land value impacts.
  • Public/private support: CAP schemes, eco/AE schemes, carbon/biodiversity/ES payments; barriers & market use of SOILCRATES data.

Living Lab Profiling & Impact

Experience with the LL

  • Interaction modes; usefulness for decisions; which decisions changed (crop/seed/land prep/inputs/pest).
  • Community sharing.

Future engagement

  • Interest in continued training/support; preferred support types & communication channels.

Demographics

  • Name, age, gender, farm/village, phone, GPS, household members.

Conclusion

  • Farmer questions/suggestions; next steps; access to deliverables/articles.
Fieldwork Checklist
  • Consent materials (information sheet, informed consent) ready (GDPR).
  • Pilot-tested questionnaire (Irish LL or local LL).
  • Enumerator training guide & coding book.
  • Data model aligned with WP4/WP5.

WP5.3 Ecosystem Services (ES) Screening & Indicators

Goal: translate field practices and farm outcomes into ES signals for prioritisation and monitoring. （目标：将田间实践与产出转化为 ES 信号，用于优先级与监测）

Scope & Objectives

  • Identify priority ES relevant to each Living Lab (LL): provisioning, regulating, cultural, supporting.
  • Map WP3 variables to ES indicators; define data sources, temporal resolution, and spatial scale.
  • Provide a lightweight screening method for design-to-deployment decisions and longitudinal tracking.

ES Categories & Example Indicators

Provisioning

  • Food/fiber yield (t/ha), income by crop, storage loss %.
  • Water use productivity (yield per m³ irrigation).

Regulating

  • Soil carbon proxy (practice-based scoring: cover crops, reduced tillage, organic amendments).
  • Erosion control (ground cover %, residue cover, slope risk).
  • Water regulation (infiltration proxy via practices; irrigation timing/efficiency).
  • Pest regulation (IPM adoption; pesticide intensity index).

Supporting

  • Soil health composite (pH in optimal range, organic matter trend, biodiversity-friendly practices).
  • Crop diversity index; rotation length.

Cultural & Socio-economic

  • Training uptake; LL engagement frequency; perceived usefulness.
  • Access to ES payments (carbon/biodiversity/water) and barriers.

Data Sources & Linkages to WP3

  • Direct from WP3: practices (cover, tillage, inputs), yields, costs, irrigation timing/costs, pest control choices, LL engagement, payment schemes.
  • External/WP4: soil lab tests, remote sensing (cover/NDVI), biodiversity surveys where available.
  • Derived: scoring indices (e.g., pesticide intensity, management intensity, soil carbon practice score).

Screening Method (WP5.3)

  1. Define LL ES priorities (stakeholder workshop or prior WP5.2 outputs).
  2. Map indicators from the WP3 dataset to each priority ES; identify gaps.
  3. Score & normalise indicators to 0–1 scales; specify directionality (higher is better/worse).
  4. Weighting (optional): apply LL-specific weights; document rationale.
  5. Aggregate to ES sub-scores and an overall ES screening index (with uncertainty bands if possible).
  6. Classify (e.g., Red/Amber/Green) to support decision-making in LLs.
  7. Report dashboards and farmer-level feedback; track change over seasons.

Outputs

  • Farmer-level ES screening report (PDF/HTML) with RAG classification and key drivers.
  • LL dashboard showing trends across seasons and comparison across practices.
  • Data package aligned to WP4 schemas for interoperability.
